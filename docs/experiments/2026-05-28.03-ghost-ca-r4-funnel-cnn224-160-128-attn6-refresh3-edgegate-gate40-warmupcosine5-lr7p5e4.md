## Goal

Run a 40-epoch learning-rate gate for the depth-preserving refresh architecture at `lr=0.00075`.

This experiment brackets the useful learning-rate range after two opposing outcomes:

- `lr=0.00025` underfit under the 100-epoch budget.
- `lr=0.001` showed fast early progress but diverged after epoch 21 with severe grad-norm explosion.

The decision this experiment informs is whether a middle learning rate can keep most of the faster early optimization signal without crossing the instability threshold.

## Hypothesis Or Expectation

`lr=0.00075` may improve on the `lr=0.0005` gate curve while avoiding the epoch-22 collapse observed at `lr=0.001`.

Expected positive evidence:

- sampled validation at epoch 40 better than the `lr=0.0005` gate value `0.6062`
- no NaN, divergence, or sustained validation degradation after warmup
- no severe grad-norm instability comparable to the `lr=0.001` run
- ideally best sampled validation by epoch 30 competitive with the `lr=0.001` pre-collapse curve

## Key Characteristics

- Model variant: `funnel-cnn224-160-128-attn6-refresh3-edgegate`.
- Architecture is unchanged from the depth-refresh learning-rate ablations.
- Training recipe is unchanged except `lr=0.00075`.
- `eta_min` is intentionally kept at `0.000001` to isolate the base-LR change.
- This is a 40-epoch gate run, not a full promotion run.
- If the gate is promising and stable, the follow-up is a 100-epoch full run with the same LR.

## Verdict Rationale

Pass. The run satisfied all pre-registered gate criteria.

The epoch-40 sampled validation loss `0.6047` beats the `lr=0.0005` gate value `0.6062` and satisfies the strong pass criterion `<= 0.6058`. The run remained stable through epoch 40, avoided the epoch-22 divergence seen at `lr=0.001`, and the grad-norm distribution was normal after early startup spikes.

This is strong evidence that `lr=0.00075` is a better optimization setting than `lr=0.0005` for this architecture under the current warmup-cosine schedule.

## Issues And Anomalies

- Early startup grad-norm spikes were present, with max `88.19409942626953`, but they were limited to early global steps and did not cause divergence.
- Full validation BCE `0.605087` should not be compared directly against 100-epoch full-run BCE values; this was a 40-epoch gate checkpoint.
