## Goal

Compare activation effective-rank profiles for the latest k-only wide-fusion model and the previous q+k wide-fusion reference.

The decision this should inform is whether the k-only variant's small accuracy regression is visible as lower representational usage or branch/fusion bottleneck behavior.

## Hypothesis Or Expectation

The k-only wide-fusion model may show lower effective rank in the global attention branch or fused representation than the q+k reference, consistent with losing some content-dependent edge-gate expressivity.

## Key Characteristics

- Analysis command: `cpe analyze-rank`.
- Dataset: `data/processed/validation.chesseval`.
- Sample policy: first `2000` validation rows, no shuffle.
- Rank threshold: singular values greater than `1%` of the largest singular value.
- Compared checkpoints:
  - q+k wide fusion: `ghost-ca-r4-256ch-parallel-cnn3-attn3ffn64-fuse1x1-nomaterial-warmupcosine5-lr5e4-100e-best`

Key observations:

- All shared, local, and global trunk blocks remained full-rank under the current `1%` threshold for all three checkpoints.
- The q+k and k-only wide-fusion models both compress at the `fuse` layer, but k-only wide fusion had a higher fuse effective rank: `208/256` versus q+k `193/256`.
- The k-only full-test BCE regression is therefore not explained by a simple activation-rank collapse in the global branch or fuse output.
- The k-only late-evidence model also had full-rank local/global branch activations, but compressed each branch to `2/2` evidence features before the value MLP. That supports interpreting the earlier failure as an interface bottleneck rather than upstream branch underuse.
- `global_block_2` had the lowest tail singular-value floor among trunk blocks in all three variants, suggesting the last attention block is the most compressed part of the branch even when its effective rank remains `256/256`.

## Verdict Rationale

Informational. There was no pass/fail criterion; this analysis was used to inspect representation usage.

The rank profiles do not support a "k-only loses because it has lower effective rank" explanation. k-only wide fusion actually used a higher effective rank at the fuse output than q+k wide fusion. The small q+k accuracy advantage is more likely due to better content-conditioned edge scoring, not a larger or less collapsed fused representation.

The late-evidence result reinforces the previous conclusion that compressing local/global branches to two evidence scalars is too narrow, even if the branch activations themselves remain high-rank.

## Issues And Anomalies

None observed.

## Follow-Up

- For the q+k versus k-only gap, inspect edge-gate activation/statistics rather than only rank.
- Consider q-only wide-fusion as the symmetric ablation if the edge-gate source question remains important.
- If improving parallel fusion, focus on fusion semantics and branch alignment rather than simply increasing rank.
